A Complete Recounting Of Some Tea-Inspired Chair-Sitting

by Elliott Noel, (C) 1996

Throughout the day I sit,
here in my favourite chair,
ingesting cookies and crackers,
sipping my English breakfast tea.
I ponder what is meant by this
self-induced activity:
Acknowledging the human desire, the
need for solitude and self-examination,
and having noticed the auto-recursive
character of such activity, I
realize that I am merely a well
oiled cog in a greater whole.
Such are the constraints of
this supposedly free form.
It is, however, possible for us to leave the
closed system if we succeed in
perceiving ourselves from the
outside of this entity. We must
emancipate ourselves of this
mechanical rigidity.
