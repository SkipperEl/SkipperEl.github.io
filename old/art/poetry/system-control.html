System Controlled

(C) Elliott Noel, 1996


Like a vile disease it spreads
To every city and town all over the world
In every country - for it knows not any borders
A new religion waging a holy-war
It's a battle cry feared by many yet welcomed
The inevitable destiny wrought by progress.

I stop at the same traffic lights day after day
I live in the system through the morning and afternoon
Enduring because I am supposed to endure
Wasting effort and time for no reason
Other than to be someone's servant
The destiny which we are supposed to accept.

I observe future managers and technocrats vying for position
Each one wanting to be the next peon
Competing against each other so that they may be
A bigger piece of the system - yet a piece nevertheless
Substance does not matter - only system
Self-sustaining and meaningless.

The system is all around us, in many forms
We live within it as if it's a natural formation
Where has the meaning gone?
Why have we lost our direction?
I want no part in this.
